Odd Mammals Region

Flying Squirrel vs. Tarsier

Last email I mentioned my friend Lance "Butterfinger" Van Houten and his love for all things "snarfy" (I don't know, it's just a thing Lance likes to say I think). When Lance was in Law School at the University of Iowa he had a pet iguana. I don't remember its name, so we'll call him Mr. Snarfy. One winter day he had Mr. Snarfy in his car, presumably for...companionship? Anyway, Mr. Snarfy got cold, as reptiles do in an Iowa Winter, and decided to seek out some warmth...in the steering column of the car. The only thing sticking out was its tail. I'm not sure how Lance got Mr. Snarfy out (he did; there were no Mr. Snarfys hurt in the creation of this story), but maybe he drove around until the rest of the car got warmer than the steering column, enticing Mr. Snarfy to emerge? You know, with the floor heat on max and only driving straight ahead so as not to cause an Unspeakable Tragedy. Maybe he took the car to a shop and said "uhhhhh....yeah....I've got a little problem with my steering column....it's uhhhh....occupied." Clearly I lack a full knowledge of this entire story, but nevertheless I wanted to share it because sometimes, when I need a little pick-me-up, I think about that iguana tail sticking out of the steering column of Lance's 1989 Toyota Corolla. TARSIER WINS!

I am relieved he's been eliminated from the potential winners, because pool veteran John Housenga selected the Pearson's Chicken Dinner Bar. There is an excellent history of this confection available that I encourage you all to read (WGNBT). I will also add that this is a candybar Time magazine ranked #6 most influential candybar of all time. From wikipedia:

The Chicken Dinner Bar had been a product of the Sperry Candy Company, which was acquired by Pearson's in 1962. The bar, introduced during The Great Depression, was so called in reference to President Herbert Hoover's promise of "a chicken in every pot". The bar did not contain chicken or other poultry products, but was, rather, a chocolate-covered nut roll. Pearson's discontinued the bar's production after the acquisition. Early TV commercials sang "Chick - Chick - Chick - Chick - Chicken Dinner" similar to, and in the cadence of a rooster crowing.

I'm kinda disappointed it didn't have actual chicken in it.
