Viral Region

I am happy to report that Covid-19 was immediately sent home by the Unstoppable Force of BBC Kid. I know many of you watched all the provided links in the bracket (and if you haven't, get on it. WGNBT), but have another look. She's the best.

The Shark Battle was a tough one. IT'S A SHAAAARK and Stuff Summer are both absolute classics in my book, but in the end I'm giving the nod to the priceless facial expressions of the Australian Today show hosts in Stuff Summer. I think if this show was just dropped in as a replacement to the American Today Show the ratings double overnight. Here's another Today Show classic:

"Well um, we'd been down at Options Tavern at a Stubbies n Singlets Party and ah got dropped off by a mate up the road and wanted to walk down the servo n get some noodles and ah, went to jump over a sign on the way and yeh slipped over and busted mah plugga."

The remaining matches had Duck Army beating FR E SH A VOCA DO and Gangnam Style over Baby Shark. Apologies to the Baby Shark faithful (171 picked it! WAT??), but Gangnam Style still, as the kids say, "slaps."

Food Region

Chocolate lost to Peanut Butter. It was tied 286-286 after 26 overtimes (!), setting a record...well, every record. It ended up being decided by an accidentally painful 1926 Libery Dollar coin flip. With Chocolate's run sadly cut short, I want to make sure everyone sees at least the opening minute of Episode 68 of Tamil-language soap opera "Chocolate". It's amazing. So many expressions! It's tense! Now it's funny! I don't know what to think! Nobody does telenovellas like...Sri Lanka? (¯\_(ツ)_/¯)

I think every episode includes 30-60 seconds of quick edit facial expressions with Dramatic Music and No Talking. Honestly, if someone put together a supercut of just these from each episode (and we know there are at least 68 episodes!), I'd totally watch that. WGNBT.

Bacon beat a plate of peas. Does this surprise you? For those that picked A Plate of Peas (47! FORTY. SEVEN.), here's a link to one of my favorite recipes, Crispy Tortellini with Peas and Prosciutto. It's superduper delicious: mint and peas and lemon compliment so nicely together, crème fraîche makes it lucious with its creamy diacritics. Really, I hated peas as a kid, but I'm now decidedly Pro Pea (and I vote). But. Sorry peas, you just aren't going to beat bacon. They have festivals for bacon. Call me when Pea-a-palooza happens, and I might re-think.

Cheese Curds earn a strong victory over Cheesecake in round 1. The bracket pundits were amazed at Cheese Curds low #11 seeding, so this was an Upset Special in the making. For those not from the Midwest and somehow not aware of these delicious cheese nuggets, they are really Quite a Thing. When fresh, they squeak when you eat them. When fried, they are a magic packet of sinful, mouth-burning delight. There's a whole Buzzfeed video of tourists in Hollywood trying them, and some of them really were disgusted. These people are not to be trusted.

Tacos v Nachos was a tough one. On the one hand, Nachos are really something. The cheese is required to be melted. That's a real point in the Nachos column alone. They involve chips. Who doesn't love chips? But, as Joe Grover eloquently put it:

Tacos come in their own packaging. You can't really beat that.

Tacos win in a nailbiter*.

* Don't bite your nails! Don't touch your face!
