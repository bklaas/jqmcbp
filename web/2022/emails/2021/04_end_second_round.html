Which brings us finally to the MEAN GREEN.

There are competing anecdotes about the origin of The North Texas Mean Green. The first one, chronicled in meangreensports.com, describes superfan Sidney Sue Green cheering on North Texas in 1966 coining the term via her cheers:

We were way behind and she started hollering, 'Come on green, get mean,' or 'Here we go, mean green,' or variants on that. Then it became popular with some of the students and some of the other fans. So it caught on.

This story is misinformation brought on by an evil cabal lead by Meangreensports, the government of Venezuela, and probably some sort of pizza restaurant. IT IS FALSE AND AWFUL AND SHOULD BE CONDEMNED.

The real origin of Mean Green, from Wikipedia:

The name "Mean Green" was adopted by fans and media in 1966 for a North Texas football defensive squad that finished the season second in the nation against the rush. That school year, Joe Greene, then a sophomore at North Texas, played left defensive tackle on the football team

They literally named their school's team after Mean Joe Greene. That is is so awesome.
