Candy Bar of the Day

where I single out one or two of my favorite candy selections

WOW. There's always a few people trolling for hard-to-buy candy bars to stump me. Some of those choices have even been won by past champions: Dulce De Leche M&M's, only available while being test marketed in certain Southwest US hispanic neighborhoods. The Melo Bar, a liquer-filled Carmelo Anthony signature candy bar available only in Denver area King Soopers grocery stores. Finally last year I needed to use eBay to find a dessicated, hope-he-didn't-eat-it 20 year old Kirby Puckett bar. But this candy is next level on the Troll-O-Meter: To'ak Chocolate, fine chocolates from the forests of Ecuador. From the website:

Made with the care of a vintage winemaker and precision of a premium small-batch whisky

Hmmm, that sounds pretty good. I like wine and whisky, so why not some craft chocolate to go with it? Sign me up!

A series of sensory impressions that tell a narrative of the origins of a chocolate and its journey from earth to tree to bean to bar.

I think I'm still interested, but this is getting a wee bit pretentious. Okay, I'll order some.

Shopping Cart: Vintage 2014 Spanish Elm × 1 $315.00 Vintage 2014 Cognac Cask × 1 $345.00 Rain Harvest 2015 Dark × 1 $270.00 Rain Harvest 2015 Light × 1 $270.00 Total $1,200.00

WAT?!?! $1200 for FOUR chocolate bars? Does it come with a fully functional golf cart? Are the wrappers lined with a sum total of $1190? No? Okay, count me WAY OUT then.

Impressively, three different JQMCBP participants went full To'ak on me: Michael Wooley-Ousdahl, Nikhil Bhandarkar, and current front-runner Ken Mills. Ken even specified he wanted the To'ak Piedra de Plata 2014 vintage* bar, which goes for about $245. Why aim so low Ken? Next time go for the Cognac Cask!

* Chocolate, like milk, peanuts, and sushi, should not have a "vintage"
