Fun Facts About Farleigh Dickinson

For the second time in tournament history (the first being the Maryland-Baltimore County Retriever's victory over Virginia in 2018), a #16 seed has upset at #1 seed. Going into Farleigh Dickinson's game versus Purdue, the collective record of #16 seeds in the tournament was 1-151. Well, now it's 2-151. Upsets like this leave us with questions, most notably, what is a Farleigh Dickinson? How did they get here? How did I get here? Rest assured, I am here for you with all the answers.

What is a Farleigh Dickinson?

Farleigh Dickinson is a rare condition that makes one's left leg, and only the left leg, involuntarily kick Rockettes-style in times of stress. If you suffer from Farleigh Dickinson Syndrome, the good news is that help is on the way. Kixatrex, a new medication proven to be effective* in the treatment of Farleigh Dickinson, can help you stop kicking your left leg in stressful situations.

* In 86% of patients, Kixatrex may cause the following side effects: sleeplessness, drowsiness, loss of appetite, incredible appetite, feelings of inner peace, excessive shouting, periodic but predictable ability to communicate with inanimate objects, spontaneous extra limb regeneration, and in rare cases perfectly moisturized skin. Ask your doctor is Kixatrex is right for you.

But seriously, let's talk about Farleigh Dickinson

  • Farleigh Dickinson had to play Texas Southern in a play-in game to make it into the 64-team field. Texas Southern's record was 14-20, yet were considered the favorites in the game.
  • Farleigh Dickinson's automatic berth in the tournament was not because they won their conference. The automatic berth defaulted to them because the conference champs, Merrimack, were not eligible as they haven't been in Division 1 long enough yet to qualify.
  • Farleigh Dickinson lost to the following teams this year:
    • Southern Illinois-Edwardsville
    • Longwood
    • Stonehill
    • Hartford
    • Queens College
    • Central Connecticut
    • Sacred Heart (twice)
  • Farleigh Dickinson is the shortest team in NCAA Division 1 basketball.
  • Farleigh Dickinson beat Purdue, who have a 7'4" center.
  • Farleigh Dickinson's head coach is an alum of Interstate 35 High School, which apart from being hilariously named, is also a school I know well. I once competed in Speech Club (known in pretty much the rest of the known universe as Forensics) at I-35 H.S. in Truro, Iowa. My performance was in the Comedic Acting section (what'd you expect, drama?), which didn't get rolling until mid-afternoon. I came down on a bus with all the other speech club nerds, including long-time JQMCBP entrant Joshua "Phil" Littrell. Josh I think was also in comedic acting? I mean, he's basically always comedic acting; ask his kids about how he dresses up in a turkey suit just to jump scare them when they wake up. But I digress. The two of us had the whole day to burn, and we chose to leave I-35 H.S. and wander the mean streets of Truro. We discovered Truro is basically the real-life representation of the song Pink Houses. I-35's team is aptly called the Roadrunners. Where am I going with this? Do I have to be going somewhere with this? I don't. I'm the Meister, and this is my party.
