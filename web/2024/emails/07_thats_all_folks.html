Losing Mascots: Let's Talk About Them!

Pointless Discussion about Losing Teams and Their Mascots

Miami University teams are known as the Hurricanes. Smartly, Miami decided their mascot would not be a giant destructive tropical storm. Granted, this would be a high concept costume that if you could pull off would be quite a thing. But we have to remember that mascots are...you know, for kids (I'm looking at you Iona Gael).

According to deeply revered internet authority fanbuzz.com,

The Ibis became the official mascot when the school yearbook took the name "The Ibis" in 1926. In 1958, John Stormont first performed at Miami football games in an Ibis costume that was "glued, sewn and pinned together."
