Well Folks, It's UCOW Time!

The Unweighted Chance Of Winning or UCOW! tells you if there's still a chance.

What's UCOW you say? For those not in the know, UCOW, pronounced at a high-pitched yell "YOU COW!," stands for Unweighted Chance Of Winning. After the field of teams is whittled down from 64 to 16, the number of remaining bracket permutations drops from, I am not making this up, > 9 quintillion (2 to the 63rd power) to 32,768 (2 to the 15th power). UCOW simulates every one of those 32,768 possible prmutations and determines the winner or winners of each permutation. After every permutation is run, results are compiled to show each person (and chimp) just how many of those 32,768 permutations they emerge victorious.

UCOW, as I note every year, is not popular with anyone. Something about knowing one's fate totally destroys morale in a complete and crushing fashion. Nevertheless, here are the results!

Losing Mascots: Let's Talk About Them!

Pointless Discussion about Losing Teams and Their Mascots

The Furman Paladins. Paladin is a word likely not familiar to many of you, but it is very familiar to one likely over-represented group in JQMCBP: those that once played (or perhaps are still playing) Dungeons & Dragons. The Paladin was a righteous subclass of Fighter character in the game, one who crusaded for a Higher Power. I don't think it was the religiosity of the Paladin that drew D&D players to it, it was...the SWORD. The +5 HOLY AVENGER was the most coveted sword in the game. Sure, the +5 HOLY AVENGER was way too unwieldy to be reasonable, but srsly it's not like you had to actually carry the +5 HOLY AVENGER. You're playing D&D! You're just a 13-year-old kid in a basement getting jacked up on Mountain Dew and Nutty Bars at 2am with your buddies, and darned if you're not gonna swing that 60-pound +5 HOLY AVENGER through those heretic orcs and hobgoblins like a hot knife through butter. THE PALADIN CANNOT BE STOPPED!

Unless of course the Paladin is facing San Diego State, in which case you will lose by 23 points.
