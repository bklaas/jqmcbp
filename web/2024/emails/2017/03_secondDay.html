Whahappened?

As The Candymeister and administrator of the World's First and Probably Only NCAA Candy Bar Pool, I get a front row seat to the raw drama* of seeing people's fates rise and fade. This year was no exception. At about 9pm Central Daylight Time, Rick Tetlow had prognosticated 28-for-28 correctly** and needed just the last four games to fall his way for a first in JQMCBP history: a perfect clean sweep of the first round picks. Really, it's never happened before. Even I, jaded and troll-like in my old age, was enthusiastically on #teamtetlow. Alas, like all good things it had to come to an end***. Rick went only 2-for-4 in the last 4 games, and fell back into a 6-way tie for first place. More on the leaders below the fold.

* The raw drama of mass dice rolling.

** Twice as many consecutive correct picks as that Rennie guy. Remember him? Yeah, me neither. Ya dead ta me, Rennie!

*** And bad things. Like the Star Wars prequels. Episode I does eventually finish. Or so I am told.

The Me. Goodbar, You Jane Award goes to Brett Rondeau for his inadvertent choice of Me. Goodbar. From Brett:

So when I filled out my bracket on my phone I accidentally hit the e instead of r so my candy bar reads "me. Goodbar" [instead of Mr. Goodbar]. I wouldn't normally care except I'm on the leader board. Can you change that for me? My next question is that if I actually win can I take the old cd from your kid instead of a candy bar? Unfortunately the JQMCBP Bylaws clearly state this is not possible.

Rule 35 Sec. 53b:
No takebacks.*

Brett, if you win, you're not going to get a CD, you're not going to get a Mr. Goodbar, you are going to get a Me. Goodbar**. You're gonna take a bite and reflexivly shout "ME. GOODBAR! ME. LIKEY!" So it is written.

* Unless you are a mom that mistakenly entered "Hershey's Kiss", "Starburst", and "Reese's" for your three children, when they (obviously) wanted "Big bag of Hershey's Kisses", "Smarties", and "Nerds", respectively (true story). There is a specific provision (Rule 35 Sec. 53c) that covers this.

Rule 35 Sec. 53c:
Respect Mothers.

I don't want to break apart families. My heart is not completely made of coal, just mostly made of coal.

** This whole "Me. Goodbar" thing reminds of a story. It has nothing whatsoever to do with basketball, but then again neither does most of this insanity. A number of years ago my oldest daughter Carson had a 2nd grade project called the "All About Me" tunnel book project. A tunnel book is a three-dimensional art project that's kind of like a paper-based diorama. But that's not important. What's important was that I read the note from her teacher describing it as the "All About Me Tunnel" book project. I thought it was a typo and she meant to say My and not Me, and that the project involved some fictitious tunnel. So that's weird. But furthermore, I thought the typo was HILARIOUS, because I read it Eliza Doolittle style, as in "yeah, dat's roight 'enry 'iggins, I'll tell ya awwwl about me tunnel, I will!" You have no idea how much mileage we've gotten from the All About Me Tunnel Book snafu of 2011.
