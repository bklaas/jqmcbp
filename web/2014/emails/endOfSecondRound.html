It was a bracket busting kind of weekend, with many of those with dreams of candy having them shattered at the hands of teams like the Flyers of Dayton, the Huskies of UConn, and the Cardinal of what I can only guess is some kind of vocational school.

This is the time of the tourney when the Hall of Shame starts filling up, and this year is no exception. 25 have officially been greeted by the Hall's Spokesclowns, the Insane Clown Posse. Amongst those Shamed are Joshua "Tangy Taffy" Nisenson and several others with what look like completely reasonable brackets. Unfortunately if your Final Four is Syracuse, Villanova, Wichita State, and Creighton, well...

Last year the Useless Statistics Division at JQMCBP HQ introduced a new and exciting statistic, the Unweighted Chance Of Winning, or UCOW (pronounced, in an accusing and shouting fashion, "You Cow!"). UCOW is a simulation of all 32,768 permutations still possible, calculating the scores and pool winners for each possibility. Pooling these data, I compiled a quick look at who has a good chance (almost none of you), who has a chance (some of you, and a suprising number of chimps), and who doesn't have any chance at all (most of you. and me.). The top 25 are directly below, and the full list is at the bottom of the email. My favorite is Deanne Busta, who will win exactly 1 out of 32,768 permutations. So you're telling me there's a chance!

One of these days I'm going to do some exciting data visualizations with these data. That day is not today.
