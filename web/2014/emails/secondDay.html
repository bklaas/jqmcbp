Upsets...who picked 'em

Highlighting those brave souls that chose oddball longshots to win games

The day started out with a bang with Duke exiting at the hands of Mercer (who both looked great and apparently had some guy on the team dancing something called the NaeNae). Only 15 people picked Duke, including Brian Stromquist who used this javascript method and this coder's bracket generator tool to generate a bracket based on weighted probabilities given by Nate Silver's published model (though notable: Silver's Mercer win probability was listed at 7%; sometimes you roll weighted dice and they still come up with the unexpected).

Also picking Duke to lose were Blair 'Beefcake' Brownyard who, as mentioned in the last update, made all of his picks based on mascot toughness. Also picking the Mercer win was at least one child under 10. Also picking a Duke loss was someone named "Candy Chatawanich". Also that candy-hating Wilbur Wonka, DDS. So...yeah.

Nothing else really ranked to high on the JQMCBP upset meter. 186 people had Stephen F Austin winning, which one in spectacular fashion with a little help from a questionable 4-point play at the end of regulation.
