Once again, the Quest for Candy is off and running!

For the rookies: What I do is send out email updates (personalized just for your entry!) several times during the course of the tournament. I go on and on and on over seemingly meaningless minutia. It might seem meaningless but...well okay, it's meaningless. But we'll have fun, or your money back.*

Pool veterans will notice that after nearly two decades of holding on to the low-tech power of plain text emails, this year's communiques are being delivered in shiny HTML. There were some practical reasons for doing this, but also has the added benefit of allowing me to sprinkle links around for your (okay, my) enjoyment. For example, the special link at the very top, where each email will have the XVII in the banner linked to XVII-related music. Often bad.

In addition to the emails, there's lots of important, vital resources available with your web browser. Check out the linky links at the bottom of the email or head to The Leaderboard as a jumping off point.

My own pool entry started off abnormally white hot today, and I was perfect after 9 games had been played before coming back to Earth. Jake the Chimp also proved that it's remotely possible for a coin to be flipped with the right prediction 13 times in a row, but Jake missed some games late and, well, has Tulsa as his champion. But Kai Anderson of D.C. (yes, THE Kai Anderson) was the lone person to make a clean sweep 16-for-16 predictions to start Day 1. Well done Kai.

As I did last year, I'm now sending emails through the appropriately named MailChimp mass email service. This is Lucky the Chimp's favorite email service, and because it actually sends emails that people tend to receive. MailChimp will not allow me to send multiple emails to the same email address. So if you signed up several brackets on one email address, you only be getting one personalized email for each entry. How about we call this a "feature" and call an end to the tech talk.
