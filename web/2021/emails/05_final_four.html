A little backstory. In 2011 the NCAA decided that 64 teams was not enough teams. So they created "The First Four", four games played before the main bracket of 64, with the winners advancing into the traditional 2^6 binary tree.

As a programmer, especially one that spent I'm Not Telling You hours coding that 64-team bracket, my decision to just completely ignore the First Four was a sane one. Since Candy Seekers needed to put in their picks before those games were played, I would just put both teams in for the entry.

For example, if Greendale Community College was playing Stratton College, I'd put Greendale/Stratton in the bracket entry. After that game was played (The Greendale Human Beings won in OT), I'd fix it in the JQMCBP database, giving anyone that selected Greendale/Stratton as a winner the benefit of the doubt that they'd really just meant Greendale.

So. An interesting thing happened this year. One of the First Four games was Michigan State/UCLA. UCLA won, and then just kept winning. Amazingly, as John pointed out, "UCLA" was the 8th most commonly selected champion, despite being seeded by the NCAA somewhere between the 44th and 47th best team.

So what happened? The thing is, JQMCBP is KIND OF A BIG DEAL in Michigan. While many of the Michigander Faithful are Wolverine fans, there are also a fair number of Michigan Staters. And 10 of them thought Michigan State UCLA was going all the way. Of the 10 people that selected Michigan State UCLA, 8 reside in Michigan.

I reached out to one of the other two, Dr. Andrew Nicholson of Colorado, to get some clarification on his selection. He shared SO MUCH good intel, especially the bit about giving Earvin Johnson a parking violation.

Don't get me wrong, Eagles are huge and awesome, I just think Oral could have dreamed bigger. 3 out of 5 stars. It was really fun watching their team though, this 4th place regular season Summit League team was legit.
  • Crimson Tide - I've gone off on this many times in year's past. There's nothing more terrifying than a Red Tide, a toxic floatilla of deadly dinoflagellates.
