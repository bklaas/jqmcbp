Losing Mascots: Let's Talk About Them!

Pointless Discussion about Losing Teams and Their Mascots

I mean, I could deep dive the Billikens, but instead I think I'll give you an anecdote about Cyclones. But not the Iowa State Cyclones, instead their dimunitive counterpart the Ames High "Little Cyclones"*

The actual mascot of Iowa State is a Cardinal (which is not an ornithologically accurate depiction of a cardinal, but that's a story for another day), because its colors are Cardinal and Gold. Ames High's colors, and this is important to the story, are Black and Orange.

So back in the 1980s, in the long long ago, AHS decided it needed to have its own dressed up mascot for games. Rather than a Cardinal, which didn't make sense, even if depicted with ornithological accuracy, they went with a cyclone. Since you can't go with black, as this wouldn't be easily seen at a night football game, they went with orange. If you think about the physical structure of a cyclone, it's pretty narrow at the top and pretty wide at the bottom. If you're in that costume, as 1989 AHS graduate Robert Wilke was, your options are effectively limited to one option: spinning around.

So basically, we'd come to games and watch Robert try not to get to dizzy/sick as the World's Only Twirling Carrot.

* My junior high school (best one in Ames until they closed it down) was the Whirlwinds, a perfectly excellent on-topic mascot. Ames High could have chosen the Tornadoes, the Dirt Devils, the Twisters, or even the Dangerous Straight Line Winds. But nope, they went with "Little Cyclones". Like a tornado, only not so scary!
