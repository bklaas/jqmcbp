Whahappened?

Congratulations to Julia "GUMMY BEARS" Foster, the JQMCBP XIX CHAMPION!

If you recall, a few emails ago I was trumpeting the dominance of one 11-year-old Kiera Delargy, leading the field of Candy Seekers for several rounds in a row. This was a great accomplishment, and Julia should be proud. But alas, in the end, Kiera was JUST. TOO. OLD.

Out of the woodwork, with a special hunch about Villanova going all the way, comes Julia "I'm 10, and I'm better than all 756* of the rest of you" Foster. Julia has earned the coveted Gummy Bears prize with 112 points, outpacing the entire field by 5 or more points. Fosters: send me your address and I'll send Julia some Gummy Bears.

* +1000 Chimps

Second place, winning the traditional prize of BIG FAT NOTHING, is a tie between Brenda VB and Bill Girardot. Congrats! I do not need your address!

While the game was being played Monday, I'd sort of forgotten that Hawaii Standard Time is 5 hours behind what I'm used to. I was walking down the street in Historic Kapaa Town in the late afternoon, specifically right outside this sports bar with wide open-air windows, and spied through the TVs over the bar that there were 14.5 seconds left and North Carolina was down 3. Then UNC hit this ridiculous shot to tie up the game with 4 seconds left. Villanova calls a time out, and I continue to loiter. Then they hit a buzzer-beater to win the thing.

After much whooping and hollering by myself and the 4-6 other people paying attention in Historic Kapaa Town*, I proceeded down the road and announced loudly to several people with heads buried in smartphones that Villanova had pulled it out (I was right, they were all checking scores). One of the more fun endings to an NCAA championship game, and in case you missed it check out this tremendously entertaining set of every angle and every call of the buzzer-beating Villanova shot. My favorites are the Canal+ Spanish announcer Antonio Lamolda "Siiiiiiiiiiiiiiii! Viya-Nova!", the Villanova IMG announcer Ryan Fannon "CATS WIN! CATS WIN! CATS WIN!", as well as the contrasting Charles Barkley / Kenny "the Jet" Smith reactions.

* three of which were my wife and daughters
