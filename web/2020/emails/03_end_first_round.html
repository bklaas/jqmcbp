Odd Mammals Region

Some of you may know, and most of you don't, that while I am a mild-mannered programmer by day, I hide a secret past: mammalogist. It's true, I used to study wee furry critters as a full-time gig. In fact, when the very first JQMCBP was launched, it was during my grad school days studying the Plains Pocker Gopher, Meadow Voles, and other assorted critters on a NW Iowa Prairie. For realsies!

But now the year is 2020, and what we care about is WHAT MAMMALS SURVIVED in the round of 64 of JQMCBP XXIII.

First up! Pangolin vs. Mountain Beaver. The Mountain Beaver is a weird, cool, and certainly ODD one. Unrelated closely to pretty much anything (not even beavers), the Apolodontia aka Mountain Beaver of the Pacific Northwest certainly checks all the boxes. Unforutnately, it went up against the Pangolin, an absurd cross between an anteater and an armadillo. PANGOLIN WINS!

Next up! The Battle of the Flyings! The Flying Fox could have been a significant organism in my professional life. True story, at the conclusion of my M.S. in Ecology I applied to work on a Flying Fox research project in the Northern Marianas islands in the South Pacific. Had I been hired...well, things would have gone quite a bit different, wouldn't it. But I didn't get that job, and therefore...FLYING SQUIRREL WINS!

What's next? Oh, Tarsier vs. Aye-Aye. They are both so. very. strange. Aye-Aye's have tremendous, extended middle fingers. Tarsier's have the largest eye-to-face ratios of any mammal. TARSIER WINS!

Last up! This one was tough. As a former rodent biologist, it's hard not to like the engergetic bipedal madness of the Kangaroo Rat. But, even harder not to like is the murderous intenstity of the Grasshopper Mouse, Killer of Scorpions, Killer of Mice, and Prolific Howler. GRASSHOPPER MOUSE WINS!

Punk Birds Region

The Punk Birds region presents tough selections. So many birds with bad attitudes! But, we need victors. Let's do this.

Australian Magpie vs. Monkey-eating Eagle

Monkey-eating Eagles eat monkeys. This is exceptionally punk and a deep misreading of the evolutionary ladder. Australian Magpies have a website dedicated to mapping attacks on people. Point in the magpie's favor. Also, and I shouldn't need to remind you of this, THE EYES DON'T WORK THE EYES DON'T WORK GET MUM GET MUM THE EYES DON'T WORK. MAGPIE WINS!

Greater Roadrunner vs. Common Cuckoo

Common cuckoos are brood parasites, laying their eggs in other birds nests for them to raise, like the punks they are. Greater Roadrunners, which are also cuckoos, eat rattlesnakes. Beep! Beep! ROADRUNNER WINS!

American Dipper vs. Cassowary

The Cassowary is literally known as The Murder Bird, with 5" claws that it uses as a weapon that has more than once killed people. It's quite punk. The Water Ouzel, aka the American Dipper, is a songbird that has adapted to diving into fast-moving streams to catch little bugs. DIPPER WINS!

Shrike vs. Lammergaier

This was easily the hardest of the Punk Birds region to select a winner. Shrikes impale their prey, be it bugs or lizards or even other birds, on thorns or even barbed wire. It's very punk. Lammergeiers, or Bearded Vultures, eat bones. That's it, just bones. Not the flesh off the bones. Bones. They drop e.g. goat femurs from high heights in the Ethiopian mountains, shattering them on the rocks below, then picking up (still large) bone bits and swallowing them whole. They have evolved special digestive juices so acidic they dissolve the bone quickly, making for a nutritious meal of BONES. Also they paint themselves with red mud to make themselves look more badass. As if this were necessary.

I couldn't choose, so I did another coin flip. SHRIKE WINS!
