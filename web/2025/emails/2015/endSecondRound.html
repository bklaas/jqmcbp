UCOW: My God, It's Full of Data

A belated howdy-do to you all, intrepid Candy Seekers. I am usually more timely with my email updates, but this year I had the complicating circumstances of being OLD. Hey, if the excuse works for Tim Duncan it works for me...

Aaaaanyway, this is always a fun email for me, because after the first weekend we're down to 16 teams left in the bracket. That means that we've gone from an initial 2^63 permutations of possible brackets (about 9 quintillion) down to 2^15 or 32,768 brackets. Still seem like a lot? Well, not to a computer. And, given I know how to fidget with these computer things, I'm once again unleashing my Unweighted Chance Of Winning™, or UCOW™ (pronounced "You Cow"™). The UCOW™ is me simulating every single remaining permutation, calculating the winner of each of these brackets, and then publishing the summary statistics for everyone to see. UCOW™ has generated huge buzz from the analytics community:

SO. AWESOME.

-- testifies Ben Klaas

I don't know. I just...I don't know.

-- gushes Wendy Volkman

Sucks the wind out of an NCAA pool.

-- elucidates Noam Chomsky

It's kind of a buzzkill

-- raves most people

The first exciting* discovery is that the chimps are SO still in it. 6,419 or 19.6% of possible bracket outcomes emerge with a chimp victor. Granted, most of those involve Wichita State, Xavier, or NC State winning the championship, but still.

The second exciting** discovery is that the Glory and Power of the Normal Distribution has once again demonstrated that you can have an NCAA pool of > 750 entrants and be reasonably assured there is going to be one and maybe two winners of the pool. It's lonely out there on the tails. That's why I like to hang out in the middle of the bell curve, where I'm surrounded by friends (and a few chimps).

The third exciting*** discovery is that a) Victoria Hebda wins a whopping 5.4% of all brackets (that might not seem like much, but based on past UCOW™'s, it's a lot), and that the pool's over for all but about 220 of you (see above quotes with regards to lack of wind and death of buzz).

** for nerds
