SHAME

Moving on from Michigan SHAME, today we get to talk about a Very Special Subset of the Mascot population: The Vocational Mascot. Unlike the lazy Wildcats, Bruins, and Bulldogs, the Vocational Mascot has a job to do. And that job is, by gosh, to GET. THINGS. DONE.

I'm going to start with a mascot not even eligible for the NCAA Tournament, as it's a high school. Much to my daughters' chagrin, I'm going to highlight their crosstown rivals the Minneapolis Washburn Millers. There's nothing not to like about the Miller. As a mascot, he's tough and menacing, but off the court he's grinding flour for our breads, muffins, and most importantly, waffles.

The Miller is an excellent mascot. One of the best.

Now for the freshly-vanquished Vocational Mascots. Let's start with the Providence Friars. I'm not sure what a Friar is supposed to do at a basketball game, and I'm concerned his duties at the Abbey might be being neglected, but clearly one of his activities is getting hoisted in the air.

Next up we've got the Purdue Boilermaker. Just look at that hammer! What kind of boiler is he making with that? I love the work ethic on display here, but seriously I'm not going to trust my boiler work to someone with that tool.

Lastly we've got the Texas Tech Red Raider. I'm not sure what job a Red Raider exactly is, but it's not like I'm going to doubt whatever it is Red has in mind. Dude does not like to be crossed.

That was the 2018 version of Raider Red. Since then, Red has received an Upgrayedd*, receiving both eyes** and a cartoonishly large gun.***

* that's two D's for a double dose of Upgrayedd.

** Lance "Whatchamacallit" Van Houten pointed out that he considers the Saint Mary's Gael (featured in last email) as "The Windowless Van" of mascots. If the Gael is behind the wheel of said van, then the eyeless 2018 Red Raider is sitting shotgun.

*** Of course he's got a gun. It's Texas, y'all.
