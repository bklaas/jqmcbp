Losing Mascots: Let's Talk About Them!

Pointless Discussion about Losing Teams and Their Mascots

We had so many great losing mascots today!

  • The Vermont Catamounts

    • A Catamount is derived from "Cat of the mountain," which is a mountain lion. I love the colloquial and bucolic nature of the Catamount name. So perf, so Vermont.

  • The South Dakota State Jackrabbits

    • Like the Catamount, a geographically appropriate mascot. Fast, big ears, cute. But, a prey species. I might have suggested they go with Jackalope.

  • The Longwood Lancers
    • The Lancer mascot is Elwood, a horse, and the official logo features a horse's head atop a lance.

    I'm going to suggest they take a second look at their logo description there. Because EW.

  • The San Francisco Dons

    • Who's got two ginormous thumbs and name means "Distinguished Gentleman"? THIS GUY

  • The Akron Zips

    • And last but not least the Akron Zips. Where do I start? From the beginning I guess...the Zips were named after a rubber shoe, that's right a rubber shoe, manufactured by the local BF Goodrich plant. The shoes, and the team, were actually named the Zippers, but towards the middle of the 20th century it seems that the young folk of Akron were transitioning away from fastening the flies on their pantaloons with buttons, and opting instead for a new fangled invention, the "zipper". Not wanting to be associated with such a device, the university shortened their name to just "Zips".

    Now for the mascot. A kangaroo! What's zippier than a Kangaroo? Like Vermont's Catamount and South Dakota State's Jackrabbit, the woods around Akron are teeming with native kangaroos*. Zippy the Kangaroo is, true story, one of only eight college mascots in the U.S. that identify as female. You go, girl!

* they are not.
