Losing Mascots: Let's Talk About Them!

Pointless Discussion about Losing Teams and Their Mascots

OH YES IT'S XAVIER TIME.

Xavier University's mascot is D'Artagnan the Musketeer. According to Cincinnati.com, the Official Musketeer Curator of the Greater Cincinnati Area:

Reverend Francis J. Finn, a Xavier Board of Trustees member, proposed D'Artagnan the Musketeer as Xavier's mascot in 1925. According to the university's website, Finn felt D'Artagnan represented Xavier's historical connection to France and embodied the school's "all for one, one for all" spirit.
Cincinnati.com, which is a website that also has strange chili recipes that use spaghetti and oblique references to "hayrides," goes on to say this:
The mascot, however, would not make its debut until October 1965, nearly 40 years after Finn's proposal. Bill Peters, a 1968 Xavier graduate, was the first student to dress up as D'Artagnan. Peters was the mascot for a homecoming game against Villanova, in which the Musketeers defeated the Wildcats 35-0. His costume consisted of a white blouse with blue pleated sleeves, a brown vest, dark blue pantaloons, a blue cape, a fencing foil and fake brown boots.

And here he is, D'Artagnan.

Cincinnati.com, which has a standing ban on reporting anything about Cleveland, also says "The university reports that up to four Xavier students now fulfill D'Artagnan's role." Pedantically, I think these four should be named D'Artagnan, Athos, Porthos and Aramis, but I digress. What is clear here is that they definitely have enough mascots.

BUT WAIT.

Since 1985, Xavier University has TWO mascots. I know what you're asking: what could possibly compliment this suave, swashbuckling Musketeer, with his debonair cape and feathered hat?

What better foil (see what I did there) to D'Artagnan than...a Blue Blob?

That's right, Xavier's secondary mascot is a Blue Blob. Here it is cowardly poking an unexpecting D'Artagnan in the back.

Now I love a good absurdist mascot. Don't get me started about the Western Kentucky Big Red, a distant relative of this Blue Blob here. Mad props to Big Red. But...Xavier already had a very strange mascot! Isn't that enough? Why two?

Again, Cincinnati.com, a website still inexplicably dancing the Ickey Shuffle, has the goods:

The Blue Blob was created in 1985 as a kid-friendly alternative to the sometimes-scary main mascot. The costume features a muppet nose, hound eyes and, of course, blue fur.

Cincinnati.com, which apparently had no real news to report on, also produced this video on the Blue Blob.

Okay, Blue Blob. You're "for kids". I'll allow it. I only have one ask, and that's for Xavier to give Iona a call. Iona has an "always-scary" mascot problem.

 

Couldn't Blue Blob please just get traded to Iona? ¯\_(ツ)_/¯
