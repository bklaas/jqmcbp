Losing Mascots: Let's Talk About Them!

Pointless Discussion about Losing Teams and Their Mascots

I have a fascination with non-plural mascots, but we're going to have to wait on the North Texas Mean Green deep dive, because they won! Disappointed that Colgate's mascot is not Tartar Control, let's instead spend some time discussing Hokies.

VT, short for VA Tech, short for Virginia Tech, is short for Virginia Agricultural and Mechanical College and Polytechnic Institute. Which is a mouthful. It also makes me wonder, are there Monotechnic Institutes? ANYWAY, the Virginia Tech mascot is a Hokie. What is a Hokie?

The Hokie Bird is a larger-than-life turkey often seen wandering the college campus in Blacksburg, Virginia.

Well that's just positively terrifying. Have you ever been chased by a turkey? I HAVE. I can't imagine sending my child to a campus where a larger-than-life turkey is allowed to just wander around*. NO THANK YOU.

Going a bit deeper, according to extremely well known website infomory.com:

Hokie is popularly known as the castrated turkey mascot of the Virginia Tech. However, if you try to closely look into the comprehensive history and background of this term, it has no relation whatsoever with the infamous turkey. In fact, this word has "no" meaning at all. It was first used by someone who won the cheer writing competition in Virginia Tech named O.M. Stull during the school year 1896.

Oh gosh. That's a lot to unpack. Ignoring that first sentence entirely, let's start by asking why they put "no" in quotes? "What" is that all about? Also, how did that cheer go? Was O.M. using Hokie to fill out a rhyme? If so, to rhyme with "what"? Smoky? Karaoke? Gnocchi? So many questions.
