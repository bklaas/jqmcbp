Congratulations JQMCP XXV Champion!

Andy "Awesome See's Nuts & Chews Bar" Calvert

It is my great honor, or if you're British or just odd, honour, to announce our JQMCBP XXV Champion Andy "Awesome See's Nuts & Chews Bar" Calvert. Kansas's win over UNC last night gave Andy 96 points and a 1 point over Todd "whatchamacallit" Berenak, who in second place wins the traditional prize of BIG FAT NOTHING. Sonya Connolly rounds out the top 3 at 91*.

Achieving Andy has achieved a never before achieved achievement, he is a TWO-TIME JQMCBP CHAMPION. He last won in 2005**, where his candy bar was, checks notes, See's Nuts & Chews Bar. Ralph Waldo Emerson once said, "Consistency is the hobgoblin of tiny minds." Turns out, also of multiple JQMCBP champions! Andy, send me your address details and I'll send you some candy. To all others, better luck next year!

Andy's candy choice has been called in reviews, and I'm not making this up, "Where are the nuts?," and "Not enough nuts," and "Where are the almonds?" and "These have changed." Looking closer at the wrapper though, I see that it's not See's Nuts & Chews Bar but See's NUT & CHEW bar. These reviewers are getting plenty of nut. Just enough nut for one chew.

But wait, there's more! I won't end this year without giving props to the 1,000 Chimp Army! This year didn't end in victory, but it was the Best Year Ever for this vicious band of simians. Not one but two Chimps placed in the top 10! Mario the Chimp ended up in 6th place, the highest placing chimp in JQMCBP history. Mario had Saint Peters in the Final Four, and was one more Peacock victory away from running away with a delicous Krembanan. Dallas the Chimp snuck in at 9th place just one point behind Mario, and may have been the only bracket, human or chimp, that prognosticated a Kansas over UNC championship game. Also worth noting, with Mario finishing 6th this means 748 of 753 human beings could not beat a random coin flip bracket. Well done everyone!***

I hope you all had a good time with this thing. I keep doing it, twenty-five times and counting, and you all seem to keep coming back. I'll take that as a sign. Or at least a cry for help.

Cheers and see you all next year!

Pointless Discussion about Losing Teams and Their Mascots

We lost another vocational mascot last night:

The term "tar heel" dates back to North Carolina's early history, when the state was a leading producer of supplies for the naval industry. Workers who distilled turpentine from the sticky sap of pine trees and burned pine boughs to produce tar and pitch often went barefoot during hot summer months and undoubtedly collected tar on their heels. To call someone a "rosin heel" or "tar heel" was to imply that they worked in a lowly trade.

So naturally their mascot is a giant foot.
